the objective of this site

The internet is pretty good at connecting people. I love spending time with people.  So when I asked the internet to introduce me to some of its more interesting friends, it agreed happily, but on one condition: Given that I'm looking to discover good people to work with, I had to begin by making myself discoverable. This is site is my starting effort. If you're into it, please reach out. I'd love to meet you.

shared interests = shared vectors

Curiousity has always been a driving influence throughout my life. I value engagement over comfort and have come to understand and genuinely appreciate that learning is best considered a network of travel routes where, counterintuitively, the travelers level of experience is inversely proportional to the number of endpoints they've encountered.  

To use an analogue that's a bit less abstract, whenever we travel to another country for the first time, regardless of how positive or negative the experience we unavoidably add a new dimension to our perspective, increasing our global level of comprehension (pun intended).   

Continuing this analogue one final step further, experiencing the journey with companions you expect to know long term is typically preferable, as shared memories, both good and bad, result in a unique shared bond.    

All of us are nodes in our various community networks. As this necessarily subjects everyone to the mechanics of network theory, abundance is a function of contributing and sharing resources.  Hence, I want to make myself discoverable, my interests public and my digital contibutions available.  As the saying goes, you can talk about it or you can be about it. I choose both.
