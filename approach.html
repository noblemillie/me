Charting the Collaborative Landscapes

This blog effectively serves as a mechanism for me to make network request, or a series of network requests to be more accurate. The networks I’m referring to are the handful of interconnected human-networks organized around the knowledge domains of:

- web development -
- interactive data visualization -
- cognitive science -
- formal theories of language and information architecture -
- data science & statistics -

I personally view these difference domain-specific networks as a single larger network, albeit with clusters which are still in a weakly connected phase, which collectively cover govern the digital interface between human and machine.

My experience till now has been on business-side of company the various corporate functions, which I vary much enjoyed but decided I needed some first hand experience on the technical side as well, if I ever hoped to earn legitimate full-stack knowledge and global understanding of the underlying systems which constitute what’s generally referred to as the product company.

While none of these domain networks have a formal gate-keeper per se, joining a new network is effectively a function of establishing connections with at least some of the other nodes, some of whom, I hope, stumble across this blog at some point and might find the content interesting enough to want to opt-in to establishing a direct connection with me.

As a brief aside to share my personal perspective; as I see it, all networks function as a system of sharing resources and collaborating efforts. Hence, the health or net value of any network is ultimately determined by a function of contributed resources (which typically adds compound value in nature), less the marginal loss in network capacity incurred by members using/borrowing these said resources (which typically subtracts from overall resource availability in more linear fashion). 

Therefore, anyone looking to join a new network should make all possible attempts to bring some resources to share if they want a reasonable shot at integrating in any meaningful way. Hence, this blog is my humble attempt at spinning up an online repository which I hope I can populate with content someone finds useful. 

As it’s probably best to sum it up layman terms: 
-> Hello tech. My name is Noble and I'm down for the cause. I believe in collaboration over competion and distributed influence over centralized control. Thank you for allowing me to introduce myself and I encourage any kindred spirits to do the same. 

So with that, lease don’t hesitate to reach out if you find anything published here of interest. Love to help another node out wherever I can and look eagerly forward to the road ahead.

   -Noble
